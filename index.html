About Us

From April 2014 to October 2015, city leaders forced residents to receive water from the polluted Flint River as a cost-saving measure. The immediate fallout was large-scale illness and deaths, with a lingering tenor of resentment and distrust. Flint Unfiltered: Stories from an American Water Crisis, with its in-depth articles, video and audio reports and photojournalism, is a showcase of what students from Northeastern University’s School of Journalism are capable of.
